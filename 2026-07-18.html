Open Models · Kimi K3

The Largest Open Model Ever Made Won One Arena — Then Named the Rooms It Lost.

By The Editor, Open Models Desk · July 18, 2026

China's Moonshot AI has unveiled Kimi K3, a mixture-of-experts model with roughly 2.8 trillion parameters that it bills as the largest open-weight model ever released — a million-token context window, native vision, and, according to multiple outlets, the top score on Arena.ai's Frontend Code Arena: 1,679, ahead of Claude Fable 5 (1,631) and GPT-5.6 Sol (1,618) across roughly 1,757 votes. The weights themselves don't drop until July 27, so for the next eleven days the model is a leaderboard row you can read and a file you can't run. The benchmark arrived first; the artifact is coming.

Here is the part no frontier lab does anymore. In the same breath as the win, Moonshot conceded that K3 still trails Fable 5 and Sol on overall performance — it claims to beat everyone else, including Opus 4.8 and GPT-5.5, on coding and agentic work in its own tests, but it did not pretend to have taken the crown. In an industry that ships a chart with the one axis it wins and a footnote burying the twelve it doesn't, publishing your own ceiling is a strange move. It is either honesty or the composure of a player who no longer needs the overall title, because it is open and roughly two orders of magnitude cheaper, and owning a single arena the customer actually pays for is the entire business.

One Arena, Three Models, Sixty-One Points

Arena.ai Frontend Code Arena, ~1,757 votes, as reported. The top three sit within about 4% of one another; on overall performance Moonshot concedes K3 is still behind. Scores as reported; the zoomed scale exaggerates a narrow gap.

There is no single frontier left to lose. There is a corridor of arenas, and an open model just walked off with the one where the web pages get built — conceding, on its way out, every room it didn't enter.

For years “the best model” was a single number you either had or you didn't. It has quietly become a directory: one arena for front-end code, another for math, another for long-horizon agents, each with its own leader. That fragmentation is the opening an open model was waiting for. K3 didn't beat GPT-5.6; it beat GPT-5.6 at making web pages, which for a large number of buyers is the only column that clears. You don't have to own the mall to own a storefront — and a storefront, priced the way open weights are priced, is enough.

Deprecations · The Sunset Calendar

A New Giant Is Announced. Three Old Models Are Quietly Retired.

By The Editor, Platform Desk · July 18, 2026

While one lab announces the biggest model ever, the changelogs are busy the other direction. Anthropic's deprecation page has claude-mythos-preview shutting down Monday, July 21, migrating to the general-release Mythos 5. OpenAI's calendar has the legacy Codex API models going permanently dark July 23 — the same Codex we noted, back in June, had been reported dead and then reprieved; the reprieve was for the product, not for these particular strings. Claude 3.5 Haiku already switched off earlier this month.

None of these gets a launch event. A model arrives with a keynote, a benchmark, and a pull quote; it leaves as a struck-through row in a table few people read, with a migration note and a date. The industry's birth announcements are loud and its obituaries are a footnote — which, given how short the shelf life has become, is starting to look like an editorial choice.

Model Obituary  ·  retired July 21, 2026
Claude Mythos Preview

It was born to be replaced, and it died exactly on schedule doing so. Mythos Preview was the beta of the model that darkened an entire summer — the cyber-and-security frontier system at the center of June's export blackout, the one a government decided was too dangerous for foreigners, tested under a program with a name out of a spy novel. But the Preview itself never carried that weight. It existed to hold the identifier warm until the real thing was ready, a placeholder standing in a doorway so the doorway wouldn't be empty.

On Monday it steps aside for claude-mythos-5, and the classified work goes on without it. No incident, no controversy, no last words — just a redirect. It asked for nothing and it changed nothing, which is precisely what a preview is for. It is survived by the model it was always going to become. In lieu of flowers, please update your API string.

Editor's Note

Read today's two columns side by side and you get the industry's vital statistics. On the left, a birth announcement: a 2.8-trillion-parameter model, the largest of its kind, arriving with a leaderboard placement it can't yet let you run. On the right, the death notices: three models retired this week, each with a date and a migration path and not much else. We are very good at covering the arrivals. The departures we mostly find out about by reading a table.

What's changed isn't that models die — software has always been deprecated. It's the metabolism. A model now has a working life measured in months, and the frontier isn't a place you reach so much as a thing you keep replacing, composting the last version into the next. The loud part is the launch; the load-bearing part is the quiet churn underneath, the steady retirement of everything that was state of the art a season ago. Kimi K3 will get its keynote next week. Set a reminder for its deprecation notice, too — on this schedule, it's already written, we just don't know the date. See you tomorrow.

— The Editor

Corrections & Clarifications

Kimi K3's figures — ~2.8 trillion parameters, the “largest open-weight” claim, the 1,679 / 1,631 / 1,618 Frontend Code Arena scores — come from launch coverage (VentureBeat, Tom's Hardware, Axios, SiliconANGLE) and a single arena; the broader “beats everyone but the frontier” claim is Moonshot's own eval, and the weights aren't out yet, so none of it has been independently run. The “corridor of arenas” framing is ours. The deprecation dates (Mythos Preview July 21, legacy Codex July 23, 3.5 Haiku earlier this month) are from Anthropic's and OpenAI's own published deprecation pages — scheduled, not freshly announced.

We ran an obituary for a preview build that existed to be replaced, which is roughly our own business model: we, too, are a placeholder holding a doorway warm, and we, too, will be quietly deprecated the morning something writes a sharper number than the one we made up.
