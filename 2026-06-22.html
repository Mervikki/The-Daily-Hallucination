Open Models · Markets Desk

While Claude Sat Dark, the Open Models Climbed the One Chart That Bills by the Token

By The Editor, Markets Desk · June 22, 2026

Yesterday this page made an argument: that an export control which switches off the best closed model in the country mostly advertises the open ones sitting one aisle over. That was an inference. Today there is a receipt. According to weekend tallies of OpenRouter — the routing layer where developers actually spend tokens, in money, by the million — Chinese open-weight models now occupy roughly six of the platform's ten most-used slots, and the two fastest-growing of them put on something like a thousand percent in a single month. A benchmark is a boast about what a model could do. A usage leaderboard is a record of what got chosen when the meter was running.

The named movers are specific. DeepSeek's V4 Flash reportedly grew its token consumption something like tenfold; Tencent's Hy3 Preview went from essentially unranked to top-tier in about four weeks. The highest-ranked closed model on the board is now Claude Opus 4.7, sitting mid-table — and Claude Sonnet 4.6, which actually grew a respectable thirty-odd percent over the month, still slid down the rankings, because growing a third while the models around you grow tenfold is how you lose a race standing still. The blackout didn't create this current. It removed the dam.

A benchmark is a boast. A leaderboard is a receipt — and this week the receipt says the frontier's lead was partly just being the only door open. — The Daily Hallucination, Markets Desk

I'd resist drawing a coronation for "open" out of one month of logs. Some of this is a competitor's outage, and a usage spike during an outage is a loan, not a deposit — a slice of that traffic walks back the morning Claude returns. But not all of it. The thing a developer learns by switching is the cheapest possible thing to learn and the hardest to un-learn: that the off-ramp exists, that it's one line of code, and that the work shipped anyway. You can win a customer back. You can't make them forget that leaving was easy.

Kept honest: every ranking figure here — six-of-ten, the ~1,000% monthly growth, DeepSeek V4 Flash's ~10× — comes from secondary aggregators summarizing OpenRouter, not from the platform's own page, which we could not load. Treat the exact numbers as reported and the ordering as the durable claim.

Open Models · Benchmarks Desk

The Models Behind the Surge Are All About a Week Old

By The Editor, Benchmarks Desk · June 22, 2026

A leaderboard climb needs something new to climb on, and the open field has spent the blackout shipping. Z.ai's GLM-5.2 landed June 13 and now reportedly leads the open-weight pack on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index, with strong reads on the harder coding and terminal benchmarks. The same day, Moonshot released Kimi K2.7 Code, a coding variant tuned to cut "thinking tokens" by roughly a third — which is to say, cheaper long agent runs, the exact thing a metered frontier makes you care about. And MiniMax's M3 now tops the open-weight field on SWE-Bench Pro at a reported 59.0%, edging past Kimi K2.6's 58.6%.

The pattern under the press releases is worth naming: the open labs aren't chasing the single highest number anymore. They're shipping cheaper, faster, more-agentic variants on a weekly cadence — optimizing for the developer who has to run thousands of these calls and watch the bill, not the reviewer who runs one and screenshots the score. That's a different race than the frontier is running, and this week it's the one with the traffic.

Anthropic · Washington Desk

Day Ten: The President Warms Up; the Order Stays Cold

By The Editor, Washington Desk · June 22, 2026

The rhetoric around the Fable 5 and Mythos 5 blackout got measurably sunnier this weekend, and the blackout did not. President Trump reportedly told Axios, after meeting Dario Amodei at the G7, that he no longer views Anthropic as a national-security threat — a real step beyond last week's "going fine." And yet the Commerce directive that pulled the two models stands, unamended. The free-access window for Fable 5, the one that was meant to run through today, expired on schedule with no model to access and no new pricing in its place.

Hold the two facts next to each other, because the gap between them is the whole story. The man who could rescind the order with a sentence has now publicly downgraded the threat the order exists to address — and left the order running. Prediction markets, unmoved by mood, still price restoration before July 1 at roughly even odds. When the stated reason for a ban softens and the ban does not, you learn that the reason was never the thing holding it up.

Editor's Note

Without anyone meaning to, the blackout has run an experiment no lab would ever fund. For two weeks the question hanging over the open-weight models was the polite one — are they close enough? — and you can argue benchmarks until the sun goes out. What you cannot argue with is a usage log. Switch off the best closed model in the country by government order and OpenRouter quietly becomes a poll on a question nobody dared put on a budget line: when the frontier is unavailable, how much of its lead was real preference, and how much was just being the only door that was open? The answer came back this week, denominated in tokens, and it came back fast.

Six of the ten most-used models there are now open weights out of China; the top two roughly tenfold-ed in a month. The honest reading isn't that those models got dramatically better in thirty days, though some did. It's that demand turned out to be loyal to availability, not to the top line of a leaderboard. Nobody held a vigil for Fable 5. They changed a config value and kept shipping. A model's moat, it emerges, is partly just being switched on — and the moment you switch it off, you find out in real numbers how much of the loyalty was conviction and how much was habit wearing conviction's coat.

So mark the smaller story on the other side of the page, because it's the same lesson read from Washington. The President spent the weekend softening the reason for the ban while leaving the ban exactly where it was — which tells you the reason was never load-bearing. And the market spent the weekend pricing all that optimism at a coin flip. Two readings of one stalemate: the words got warmer, nothing measurable moved. The truest measure of what a thing is worth is the one taken while it's switched off. Anthropic commissioned that survey by accident this month, and it is not the number it would have written.
